Sleep Apnea Diagnosis Wearable

Team Members: Abhi Jadhav, Abinaya Lakshmanan, Neil Marks, Aiden Therien

Problem Statement

We will design a way to detect sleep apnea at home with a comfortable and accessible device for patients that suspect that they have a form of sleep apnea. To simplify the problem, Dr. Wu, our client, has already designed a time-series machine learning algorithm that provides accurate diagnoses that match traditional polysomnography (PSG) tests when given the appropriate biosignals at sufficient sampling rates. Our client requires our device to record abdominal accelerometry, thoracic accelerometry, 3-lead electrocardiogram, and photoplethysmography data at high, specified sampling rates.

Design Specifications

ECG
● 16 bits of precision.
● >= 250 Hz sampling rate.
● 3 lead measurement.
● Dynamic Range of 25 - 240 BPM.

PPG
● 12 bits of precision.
● >= 100 Hz sampling rate.
● Measurement near thoracic cavity.

Accelerometers (2)
● 12 bits of precision.
● >= 40 Hz sampling rate.
● Measurement near thoracic cavity.
● Measurement near abdomen.

Data Synchronization and Accuracy
● Aforementioned signals should report within +/- 5% of true values.
● Aforementioned signals should be recorded, started synchronously, and aligned within 5ms.

Comfortability and Usability
● Weighs less than 2 pounds.
● Fits on most people.
● Is easy to put on.
● Is cleanable.

Final Design

Overview

The final design integrates electrical, mechanical, and software designs into one device. The device is broken up into two main enclosures: one central unit on the sternum and one smaller unit on the stomach. The ECG electrode wires extend from these enclosures with the left and right chest electrodes attached to the sternum unit and the ground electrode that attaches to the left hip attached to the abdominal unit. The PPG sensor is housed on the bottom of the sternum unit, and one accelerometer is in each unit. Finally, the batteries, SD card, and microcontroller were stored in the sternum unit since the sternum is a more sturdy location for attachment than the abdomen.

Electrical Design

This device integrates three different sensors - a combined ECG/PPG sensor and two accelerometers - with an ESP32 microcontroller (TinyPICO) and an SD card. The I2C communication protocol is used to communicate between the microcontroller and the sensors to set their sampling parameters and to receive the sampled data at the set frequencies. With I2C, each sensor simply has to be connected to the SDA and SCL data buses to communicate, as well as needing power and ground inputs. The data that is received by the microcontroller is then written to the SD card using SPI communication. The entire circuit is powered by two 3V CR123 batteries with 1500 mAh. Voltage regulators are then used to power the SD card and ECG/PPG sensor at 5V and 1.8V respectively while the accelerometers and the PPG LED are powered by the 3.3V output of the microcontroller. The 5V output of the microcontroller could not power the SD card due to insufficient current giving the need for the separate regulator. For the input signals, the PPG sensor simply has to make contact with the skin, and the ECG electrodes have specific input locations on the sensor. Low pass filters are added to the ECG inputs to clean the signal as well as to add safety resistors. With the many different components in this circuit, three PCBs were created instead of one. This allowed for the PCBs to be stacked to increase the vertical profile of the device rather than the horizontal profile to allow for the PPG sensor to make better contact with the skin. Mechanical Design

The sternum enclosure consisted of a box to place battery holders, two shelves to stack the PCBs, a thin bottom layer to induce higher quality PPG readings, and input/output holes to feed wires/light pipes through. A lid for this enclosure was also created to cover the electronics and to provide switch locations. No attachment method was created for the lid, and tape was used to hold it closed during testing. Next Steps and Improvement Ideas

Due to time constraints, we were not able to fine tune the mechanical design. We intend to streamline the design by making it smaller, fileting corners, and making the construction less boxy overall. In addition, we intend to modernize the individual data collection using threading, to parallelize sensor initialization functions, to increase signal synchronization.
